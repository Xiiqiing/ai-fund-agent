# Fund Operations & Investment Framework

## Fund Structure

### Typical Renewable Energy Fund Structure
```
Limited Partners (LPs)
  │   Pension funds, sovereign wealth funds, insurance companies
  │
  ▼
General Partner (GP) / Fund Manager
  │   Investment team, portfolio management, ESG oversight
  │
  ├── Fund I (Vintage Year)
  │     ├── Project A: Offshore Wind 800 MW
  │     ├── Project B: Solar PV 200 MW
  │     └── Project C: Onshore Wind 150 MW
  │
  └── Fund II (Vintage Year)
        ├── Project D: Battery Storage 100 MW
        └── Project E: Green Hydrogen Pilot
```

### Key Fund Metrics

| Metric | Description | Typical Range |
|--------|-------------|---------------|
| **Target IRR** | Internal Rate of Return (net to LPs) | 7-12% |
| **Target Multiple** | Return on invested capital | 1.5-2.0x |
| **Fund Size** | Total committed capital | EUR 1-10 billion |
| **Investment Period** | Period for making new investments | 3-5 years |
| **Fund Life** | Total duration including exits | 10-15 years |
| **Management Fee** | Annual fee to GP | 1.0-2.0% |
| **Carried Interest** | GP share of profits above hurdle | 15-20% |
| **Hurdle Rate** | Minimum return before carry | 6-8% |

## Investment Process

### Due Diligence Checklist for Renewable Energy Projects

1. **Technical Assessment**
   - Resource assessment (wind/solar measurement data)
   - Technology selection and turbine/panel supplier evaluation
   - Grid connection feasibility and costs
   - Environmental Impact Assessment (EIA)

2. **Financial Modeling**
   - Revenue projections (PPA vs. merchant exposure)
   - CAPEX and OPEX estimates
   - Debt capacity and financing structure
   - Sensitivity analysis (price, resource, costs)

3. **Legal & Regulatory**
   - Permitting status and timeline
   - Land rights and lease agreements
   - Grid connection agreements
   - Subsidy mechanisms (FIT, CfD, auctions)

4. **ESG Assessment**
   - Environmental impact (biodiversity, visual, noise)
   - Social impact (community engagement, employment)
   - Governance (anti-corruption, compliance)
   - GRESB rating targets

## Power Purchase Agreements (PPA)

### PPA Types

| Type | Description | Risk Profile |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| **Fixed-price PPA** | Fixed EUR/MWh for duration | Low revenue risk |
| **Floor-price PPA** | Minimum guaranteed price | Moderate risk |
| **Baseload PPA** | Fixed volume at fixed price | Shaping risk |
| **Pay-as-produced** | Variable volume at fixed price | Volume risk |
| **Corporate PPA** | Direct contract with corporate buyer | Counterparty risk |
| **Merchant** | No PPA, sell at spot market prices | High revenue risk |

### Typical PPA Terms
- **Duration**: 10-15 years
- **Price escalation**: CPI-linked or fixed
- **Volume**: 70-100% of P50 production
- **Counterparty credit**: Investment grade preferred

## ESG Framework for Energy Infrastructure

### GRESB Rating System
- **Rating scale**: 1-5 stars (5 = best)
- **Assessment areas**: Management, Performance, Development
- **Key indicators**: Carbon emissions, energy efficiency, biodiversity, social impact
- **Industry benchmark**: Average score ~70/100

### UN Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) Alignment
| SDG | Relevance to Renewables |
|-----|------------------------|
| **SDG 7** | Affordable and Clean Energy |
| **SDG 9** | Industry, Innovation, Infrastructure |
| **SDG 11** | Sustainable Cities and Communities |
| **SDG 13** | Climate Action |

### Carbon Avoidance Calculation
```
CO2 avoided = Annual generation (MWh) × Grid emission factor (tCO2/MWh)

Example: 800 MW offshore wind farm
  Annual generation: ~3,360 GWh
  Grid emission factor: 0.45 tCO2/MWh (European average)
  CO2 avoided: ~1.5 million tonnes/year
```

## Market Outlook (2024-2030)

### Global Renewable Energy Investment
- **2023 total investment**: USD 500+ billion
- **2030 target**: USD 1.3 trillion/year needed (IEA NZE)
- **Key growth drivers**: Policy support, cost reduction, corporate demand, energy security
