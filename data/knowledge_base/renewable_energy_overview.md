# Renewable Energy Technologies Overview

## Offshore Wind Energy

### Technology
Offshore wind turbines are installed in bodies of water, usually on the continental shelf. Modern turbines have capacities of 12-15 MW each, with rotor diameters exceeding 220 meters.

### Key Metrics
- **Global installed capacity (2023)**: 75.2 GW
- **New capacity added in 2023**: 10.8 GW (+24% YoY)
- **Levelized Cost of Energy (LCOE)**: EUR 50-80/MWh (2024)
- **Capacity factor**: 40-55% depending on location
- **Typical project size**: 500 MW - 1.5 GW
- **Expected lifespan**: 25-30 years

### Major Markets
| Region | Installed Capacity | Growth Outlook |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|
| China | 37.9 GW | Leading global growth |
| Europe | 30.6 GW | Steady expansion, North Sea focus |
| UK | 14.7 GW | Ambitious 2030 targets |
| Germany | 8.5 GW | North Sea & Baltic Sea |
| Denmark | 2.7 GW | Pioneer in offshore wind |
| US | 0.2 GW | Emerging, East Coast projects |

### Key Risks
- **Permitting delays**: Average 7-10 years from concept to operation
- **Supply chain constraints**: Turbine and foundation manufacturing bottlenecks
- **Grid connection**: Coordination with Transmission System Operators
- **Weather windows**: Seasonal installation constraints in harsh marine environments
- **Interest rate sensitivity**: Highly capital-intensive, affected by financing costs

## Onshore Wind Energy

### Technology
Land-based wind turbines, typically 3-7 MW capacity each. Lower cost than offshore but faces land availability and social acceptance challenges.

### Key Metrics
- **Global installed capacity (2023)**: 1,017 GW
- **New capacity added in 2023**: 100 GW
- **LCOE**: EUR 25-55/MWh (most competitive renewable)
- **Capacity factor**: 25-45%
- **Typical project size**: 50-300 MW

---

## Solar Photovoltaic (PV)

### Technology
Semiconductor-based panels converting sunlight to electricity. Dominant renewable technology by new installations.

### Key Metrics
- **Global installed capacity (2023)**: 1,419 GW
- **New capacity added in 2023**: 346 GW (record year)
- **LCOE**: EUR 20-50/MWh
- **Capacity factor**: 10-25% (latitude dependent)
- **Module efficiency**: 20-24% (commercial), up to 47% (lab)

### Market Trends
- **Utility-scale dominance**: 60% of new installations
- **Bifacial modules**: Growing market share, 5-20% yield boost
- **Agrivoltaics**: Dual use of land for farming and solar
- **Floating solar**: Emerging on reservoirs and lakes

## Energy Storage

### Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS)
- **Global installed capacity (2023)**: 45 GW / 100 GWh
- **Dominant technology**: Lithium-ion (95%+ of new installations)
- **Cost decline**: ~90% reduction in lithium-ion costs since 2010
- **Typical duration**: 2-4 hours (grid-scale)
- **Key use cases**: Peak shaving, frequency regulation, renewable integration

### Pumped Hydro Storage
- **Global capacity**: ~160 GW (largest form of energy storage)
- **Round-trip efficiency**: 70-85%
- **Duration**: 6-20 hours
- **Response time**: Minutes

## Green Hydrogen

### Technology
Hydrogen produced via electrolysis powered by renewable electricity.

### Key Metrics
- **Current production cost**: EUR 3-6/kg (2024)
- **Target cost**: EUR 1.5-2/kg by 2030
- **Electrolyzer technologies**: PEM, Alkaline, SOEC
- **Key applications**: Steel production, shipping fuel, ammonia, long-duration storage
